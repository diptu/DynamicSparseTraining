## Part 1 — Tier definitions

Tiers here measure **admission probability for *this specific profile***. They do **not** measure lab quality, funding depth, or topical fit. A lab can be world-class, richly funded, and a perfect topical match — and still be *closed* to you. Those are independent variables, and conflating them is the most common way applicants waste a cycle.

---

### 🟢 SAFETY

> **Definition:** A program where your profile is **at or above** the typical admitted student. You would be surprised by a rejection. Realistic expectation: **>50% admit probability**, and you would expect at least one offer from a set of three.

**Requires:** Your background matches or exceeds the incoming cohort's median. Their applicant pool is small enough that a strong engineer with a clear narrative stands out.

**Count on this list: 0.** See Part 3 for why, and where real safeties actually exist.

---

### 🔵 TARGET

> **Definition:** A program where your profile **sits squarely inside** the admitted band. Admission is plausible, not assured — it turns on fit, the SOP, and luck of the cycle. Realistic expectation: **~20–40% admit probability each**; expect roughly one offer from a set of four to six.

**Requires:** Comparable credentials to recent admits. For an ML PhD in 2026, that typically means an MS *or* a first-author publication in a recognized venue, plus letters from people who can speak to research ability.

**Count on this list: 0.**

---

### 🟡 REACH (Achievable)

> **Definition:** **Slightly above** your current profile. Not attainable today — but attainable if a specific, achievable artifact lands in the next 2 months. Realistic expectation: **~5–15%**, rising materially *with* the artifact.

**Requires from you:**
- An **arXiv preprint** demonstrating independent research capability
- A narrative that makes your industry background an **asset**, not a gap
- Ideally: prior contact with the PI

**The artifact is the whole ballgame.** Without it, these collapse to Ambitious.

---

### 🟠 AMBITIOUS

> **Definition:** Highly competitive groups where admission requires **exceptional alignment or a genuine breakthrough** — not merely a good application. Realistic expectation: **~1–3%**, even with a strong preprint.

**Requires:** Something that makes you *unignorable* — a result the PI actually cares about, a widely-adopted open-source artifact, or a direct personal referral. A solid preprint is **table stakes here, not a differentiator**: you'd be competing against applicants who already have one.

**Worth applying to?** Yes — 1–2 of them. Lottery tickets are fine when they're cheap. Just don't build a portfolio out of them.

---

### 🔴 EFFECTIVELY CLOSED

> **Definition:** Rejection is the overwhelmingly likely outcome regardless of what you do in the next six months. Realistic expectation: **<1%**.

**Not "hard." Closed.** The gap is structural — missing credentials that cannot be manufactured before December (graduate math coursework, a research track record, an institutional pedigree their pool selects on). This is a **time** problem, not an **effort** problem.

**Worth applying to?** Generally no. The application fee and the hours are better spent building the safety tier.

## Part 3 — Where the real Safety and Target tiers live

You have no safeties **because the list contains no safety tier.** You cannot relabel your way out of that — you have to fix the list.

### 🟢 Safety — Research-master's first ⭐

The standard, well-worn route from *exactly* your position:

- **Europe (funded / low-cost):** ETH Zürich, EPFL, Max Planck (IMPRS), KTH, TU Delft
- **Canada (thesis-based, funded):** Waterloo, UBC, Toronto MSc

Converts you into a **competitive** PhD applicant in two years, instead of a rejected one in a single cycle. Not a consolation prize — a pipeline.

### 🔵 Target — Mid-tier US R1s with funded efficiency groups

Dozens of solid, **NSF-funded** labs at schools ranked ~**30–80** work on inference efficiency, quantization, and serving systems. Run the same funding search on that band.

**Your production ML/MLOps background stops being an apology there and becomes a differentiator.**

### 🟡 Reach — RA / visiting researcher positions

The **only** hiring channel that operates on a rolling, immediate basis. PhD admissions do not. **Tianlong Chen's is the most explicitly open door on this list.**

## Part 4 — The strategic error to avoid

> Applying to ten top-10 labs in December, receiving ten rejections, and concluding the plan failed.

**The plan didn't fail — the target list was never survivable.**

### Correct portfolio shape

| Tier | Count | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Safety | **3–4** | Funded European / Canadian research-masters |
| Target | **4–5** | Mid-tier US R1s with funded efficiency labs |
| Reach | **2** | Tianlong Chen (RA route), Marculescu |
| Ambitious | **1–2** | Beidi Chen, and/or Tri Dao |
| Closed | **0** | Skip them |

### Sequence

1. **Weeks 1–12** — Ship the preprint. **Nothing below matters without it.**
2. **Week 13** — Email Tianlong Chen re: RA / visiting position, preprint attached.
3. **Weeks 13–16** — Build the real safety + target tiers (run the funding search on the ranked-30–80 band and on funded EU/Canada research-masters).
4. **December** — Apply to a **portfolio**, not a wishlist.
